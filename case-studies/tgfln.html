Flash + Actionscript 3.0

The Girl From Last Night is an "R" rated interactive experience that takes you on the adventure of one of three characters as they search for the girl they met last night. The problem is, they all got a little bit too drunk and don't remember a thing. The success of the characters in their quest is based entirely on choices made by the user during the experience, though regardless of the outcome, the crude, offensive, and immature dialogue is sure to entertain.

My Task
My primary role was coding the entirety of the experience, which was done in Adobe Flash and written using Actionscript 3.0. However, each member of our four-person team was involved in the writing of the dialogue, recording and editing audio, and producing graphics for the scenes. The narrative is very large tree, with each interactions involving two or three choices, which then produce two or three more choices of their own, and so on. Because of this, each iteration of the "game" is a unique experience, and for it to work, the project demanded a lot of code; over 7,400 lines to be (almost) exact.

It took a long time and was a very involving project, but in the end I can consider it my most impressive piece of work, a fun project to work on, and certainly the most raunchy thing I've ever created. We were looking to create a funny, enjoyable narrative, while keeping it as true to it's college setting as possible. Because of this, the dialogue is riddled with offensive stereotypes, sexual innuendos, strong language, and drug references, as well as anything else that is generally viewed as "college topics."

In wondering aloud whether or not I could include this project in my portfolio for fear of it being too inappropriate, a reviewer compared the material with that of South Park, and asserted that there's a demand for this type of writing, adding that even if the content is mature, the quality of work should speak for itself anyway. So here it is. I've warned you about the mature content, so enjoy at your own risk. And if you're my mother, I would urge you NOT to view this experience.
